Feral Ranch

Do

As fast as I could, I positioned my legs under me and rested on my haunches. With one swift motion I jolted up and broke my left thumb.

"FffUck!" I screamed. But I was now able to slip my broken hand from the knott and the rope unraveled.

I ripped the cloth covering my eyes and whipped my boot knife out to cut my legs free.

The rustling was coming closer and after finally releasing my legs I whipped my head around to face the approaching attacker.

My hand shook wildly, matching the beat of my heart as I braced myself. But nothing breached the small pool of light provided by the weakening camp fire.

"Who's there?" I quivered.

No reply.

"Who's there?" I yelled.

All that I could hear was the popping of burning wood.

I stood still for what felt like hours, facing where I had last heard movement.

The fire began to die and my island of light shrank.

The brush began to rustle again, but from all angles. I desperately spun around in a futile attempt to guard myself. I was not prepared for what I saw emerge from the forest.

Now standing around the rim of faint light were bodies, all shrouded in what looked like undulating shadows so dark it was as if they were holes torn in the fabric of reality.

I could not look away. I could not fathom. I could not scream.

The fire died and they stepped forward.
